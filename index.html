Patience and
perseverance

All my life I've been told that patience and perseverance are the keys to success in many things in life, especially in the world of QA, I understand that without these skills, it will be very difficult to continue to achieve your goals, and that's why I've been working on this ability since a young age.

Problem
analysis

Ever since I can remember I was a child who always liked to solve puzzles and solve problems in mind games and as much as possible to think outside the box of course I didn't always succeed and it was discouraging but I never gave up on myself and I always continued to challenge myself and I will continue until I succeed in achieving my goals and I think this is a trait Very important for starting QA. "If I set a goal for myself then I don't give up until I achieve it!"

Cooperation and
teamwork

I have always been a person who loves to work in a team and achieve success together, sometimes crises and misunderstandings happen, but I always find a solution to these problems in order to achieve success together, because when you are in a winning team, nothing will stand in our way.

Ability to deal
with changes

Throughout my life, I have had changes, some significant and some less, but I have always found a way to manage, whether it is at work, in my studies, or in life, so no matter what happens I am always ready for anything

Ability to work under pressure and deadlines

At school, I studied CNC, and we had many projects and products to submit, I always managed to meet deadlines in submitting projects and delivering products on time, sometimes there were difficulties, but when you don't give up, you get the results you need.

learning
agility

I know how to use a combination of skills to learn, relearn, and easily adapt to changes, many times in my life there were situations where I had to adapt to the workplace or to new changes in the work/study frameworks, and I almost always succeeded in doing so in every job I worked, my understanding of how and what should be done You were always very agile, whether it was in workplaces or in the projects I used to do back when I was studying CNC and I think this is a very important element in order to advance in the world of QA and beyond.
